    • Air_Fryer Turkey and Sweet Potatoes

    Air-fryers are becoming more and more popular. Last year we tried to cook the turkey in it. The turkey had to be cut into portions to fit in our lttle air fryer but. it was so. worth it. Guests that came last Thanksgiving are begging for that delicious turkey.

    What you'll need:

    • turkey breast
    • salt
    • lemon
    • yellow onion
    • garlic cloves and powder
    • bay leaves
    • peppercorn or regular pepper
    • butter
    • chicken seasoning
    • sweet potatoes
    • olive oil
    • freshly chopped parsley (optional and can swap for chives or ciilantro)

    For 6 - 8 servings, you'd need approximately:

    • 1 (5 - 6 pound) turkey breast
      • For the Brine
    • 1/2 of a cup of salt
    • 1 lemon
    • 1/2 yellow onion
    • 3 bay leaves
    • 1/4 of a cup of peppercorns
      • For the Turkey Breast
    • 4 tablespoons of butter
    • 1 teaspoon of garlic powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon of chicken seasoning
    • 1 teaspoon of salty
    • 1 teaspoon of black pepper
      • For the Sweet Potatoes
    • 5-6 sweet potatoes
    • 4 teaspoons of olive oil
    • 3 teaspoons of salt
    • 6 teeaspoons of butter(optional)
    • 2 tablespoons of fresh parsley(optional)

    What to do with your ingredients:

    1. Gather ingredients
    2. Scrub and dry your sweet potatoes thoroughly
    3. Rub the olive oil all over them and sprinkle them with salt
    4. Use a metal skewer or fork to poke a several holes in each sweet potato
    5. Arrange them in the air-fryer bin, set the temperature for 190°C and cook them for 45 minutes
    6. Turn them after the first 20-25 minutes. They should be soft enough to prick with a fork and if they are still a little firm, add another 3-5 minutes to the timer and check again
    7. Remove the baked sweet potatoes to a platter or individual plates, split them and butter them as you like
    8. Garnish the sweet potatoes with frest choppeed herbs, as desired
    9. Mix the brine ingredients together in a container large enough to fully submerge the turkey breast
    10. Fill the container three quarters of the way with. cold water
    11. Add the turkey breats to the coontainer, weigh it down with a plate if necessary or cover it with a lid. You should allow the turkey to brine between 18 - 24 hours.
    12. Remove the turkey breast from the refrigerator and pat iit completely dry with paper towels
    13. Mix together with softened butter, garlic powder, chicken seasoning, salt, and pepper. Combine until mixed evenly
    14. Rub the buttter mixture in between the skin and the meat; and a little more on top of the bird
    15. Place the turkey breast in air-fryer, with the the breast side down in the air-fryer bin
    16. Cook for 45 minutes at 175°C
    17. Flip the turkey breast and cook for. another 30 minutes, or until top of the breast is golden brown and and the internal temperature it 75°C
    18. Remove turkey breast from the air-fryer and allow it to rest for ten minutes
    19. Remove the breast from the bone and, then slice and serve with the sweet potatoes!

    This dish takes less than an hour and a half and because the turkey is cooked in the air-fryer, and is cooked breast side down for most of the time, you will get an incredible juicy bird. The brine can be alterned to incorporate whatever you wish. I think we didn't even marinate our turkey that longer and it still came out super juicy. Just look at that turkey and tell me it doesn't look good! I couldn't eat it since I am a vegetarian but even I knew it was a game changer. It can be eaten with whatever sides you like. I wanted to add another simple recipe you can whip up in the air-fryer to compliment the turkey. Last years guests dream of that turkey to this day, but no worries becaue Thanksgiving is coming up!
