    • Avocado Chocolate Cake

    I know what you're thinking... Avocado and chocolate can not be good, at all. But don't knock it until your try it. I was vegan a few years ago and foud this intriguing recipe. I have heard of using zucchini or courgette as a substitue for egg but not avocado. Avocado is a rather strong taste to try to mask with sweetness. I was up for the challenge and this recipe is a secret weapon. Every time I reveal that I made this super chocolatey cake with just 3 avocados, no one ever believe me!

    What you'll need:

    • avocados
    • all-purpose flour
    • unsweetned cocoa powder
    • baking powder
    • baking soda
    • salt
    • vegetable oil
    • white vinegar
    • powdered sugar

    For 12-16 servings, you'd need approximately:

    • 3 avocados
    • 3 cups of all-purpose flour
    • 10 tablespoons of unsweetned cocoa powder
    • 2 teaspoons of baking powder
    • 2 teaspoons of baking soda
    • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
    • 1/4 of a cup of vegetable oil
    • 2 tablespoons of white vinegar
    • 2 powdered sugar

    What to do with your ingredients:

      For the cake
    1. Preheat the oven to 175Â°C
    2. Grease and flour a baking pan or dish
    3. In a large bowl, whisk together 3 cups of all-purpose flour, 6 tablespoons of unsweetned cocoa powder, 2 teaspoons of baking powder, 2 teaspoons of baking soda, 1/2 teaspoon of salt, and set aside
    4. In a small bowl, go. ahead and mash up one of the avocados until it is very smooth
    5. In a separate bowl, whisk together the 1/4 of a cup of vegetable oil,1 avocado(mashed up), 2 cups of water, 2 tablespoon. of vinegar, and 2 teaspoons of vanilla until everything is combined well
    6. Whisk sugar into the wet. mixture
    7. Add the wet mixture to the flour mixture all at once and whisk until it is smooth and creamy
    8. Pour the batter into th greasd baking pan or dish and place in the ven to bake for 45-55 minutes or until a toothpick or knife is inserted in the center and comes out clean
    9. Let the cake cool in the pan for 15-20 minutes and can turn calke out onto a cooling rack to cool completely before frosting
      10. For the frosting
    10. Mash 2 avocados until smooth and creamy(can use a foood processor, blender,etc.)
    11. Add 2 cups of sugar and 4 tablespoons of cocoa powder to the pureed avocado
    12. Stir until completely combined and has creamy texture
    13. Frost the cake and leet sit for a few hours before serving to allow. time for flavors to blend (can also store in the fridge)

    I made this cake for a Thanksgiving pot luck at my university, I doubted there would a variety of option for people who are vegan. I made a little not specifying that it was vegan. That cake was devoured in less that 10 minutes and none of the people who were vegan were able to try it. It was so good people couldn not taste the avocado. I made the cake again for another potluck but didn't teell anyone what I made it with. After everyone finished their slices without a single crumb left, I told them they just a vegan cake made with avocados and they were stunned. Youcan decide how sweet you want to make. I add some maple syrup sometimes(one of my secret ingredients). This cake always amazes people of how good it is. You can experiment with the topping, I tried crused oreos one year and and oreo pie crust I found at the market.I personally call these cakes Death by chocolate because it is so chocolate and fudgy. This cake is truly unforgettable.
